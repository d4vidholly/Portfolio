Peppermint OT

Peppermint OT

Brief: "I need an easy to use, responsive webpage for my Occupational Therapy business. Support Coordinators will use it to review my skillset and contact me for appointments."

Roles: UX Researcher, Graphic / UI / UX Designer, Web Developer

  • Research: Users want a clear, easy to use webpage outlining contact info, location and services. They are frustrated by other sites where this isn’t obvious. Users value building a relationship with the OT to understand their values and how they work.
  • Design: Prototyping reduced 50% of the initial content to create a clearer message. Testing showed a sticky CTA performed better than a nav bar link. Users described the tone of the page as 'friendly' and 'fresh'.
  • Outcomes: Positive user feedback, no reported issues of not finding information. Users felt they could trust the client to deliver and were encouraged to connect.

The United Project

Brief: We don't make enough use of our current landing page. We need a redesign to meet our fundraising goals for 2025."

The United Project

Brief: We don't make enough use of our current landing page. We need a redesign to meet our fundraising goals for 2025."

Roles: UX Researcher, UI / UX Designer

Key Design Features:

  • Research: Users thought landing page navigation was unclear and wanted obvious ways to get involved. Secondary research showed branded donation pages can raise 6x more.
  • Design: Conducted Information Architecture audit to reduce nav bar icons and include a Shop section on page. Used imagery and color to improve navigation on the page, strengthen brand identity and create a clearer donation message.
  • Outcomes: Testing revealed users enjoyed the 'clean', responsive update. A clearer understanding of the mission and the concept of a donation goal connected users to get involved.

Empower Autism

Brief: "We need to redesign our current landing page to help users interact with our site."

Empower Autism

Brief: "We need to redesign our current landing page to help users interact with our site."

Roles: UX Researcher, UI / UX Designer

Key Design Features:

  • Research: Mobile Users didn't trust the landing page which they felt was out of date. They found the structure confusing and weren't sure what projects were available.
  • Design: Clearer headings and increased CTAs to improve navigation. Used more appropriate imagery without text overlays. Included expectations of subscription.
  • Outcomes: Users related more to the landing page and were more likely to interact with the site as a whole.
